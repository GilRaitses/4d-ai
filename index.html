Vision & Overview

Imagine an interactive 4D model that reconstructs the evolution of glacial landscapes in the Finger Lakes region—capturing both the physical terrain and the dynamic processes that shaped it over millennia.

This platform leverages high-resolution remote sensing data (via Google Earth Engine) and physics-informed machine learning to translate existing geological maps, DEMs, and simulation models into an immersive environment. Users can explore how elliptical mounds (drumlins), moraines, and sediment layers evolve through time, test hypotheses about subglacial floods and sediment flows, and derive quantitative insights from video data.

More Details on the Vision

The project’s core is a 4D reconstruction tool that not only documents historical glaciation events but also serves as a testbed for simulating future changes. By integrating datasets from LiDAR, DEMs, and published simulation models (such as FastScape, Yuan et al. 2019, and GoSPL studies), the model provides a granular, cross-scale perspective on glacial dynamics.

In practice, the platform will enable immersive exploration—allowing users to “fly” through reconstructed landscapes, observe the evolution of landforms, and interact with simulation parameters. The end result is an intuitive interface that bridges advanced computational modeling with real-world geological processes, serving as a powerful tool for research, education, and policy-making.

Proposed Methodology

My project reconstructs and analyzes glacial landforms—specifically, elliptical mounds (drumlins)—as proxies for inferring past ice flow dynamics and nonlinear physical processes.

Extended Methodology & Approach

I begin by acquiring high-resolution 3D elevation data using LiDAR and remote sensing (e.g., Google Earth Engine). These Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) are used to accurately reconstruct glacial landforms.

Next, I integrate physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) and deep operator methods to embed conservation laws (mass, momentum, energy) into my simulations. This allows me to model key processes such as ice viscosity, sediment transport, and subglacial rheology.

The finite dataset of elliptical mounds (drumlins) permits a comprehensive audit, providing a high-fidelity reference for validating simulation outputs against field data.

Key Points:

  • 3D reconstruction from DEMs
  • Inference of ice flow structures and mechanical stresses
  • Modeling nonlinear dynamics using PINNs
  • Validation through comprehensive datasets

Workflow & Project Phases

The project follows a phased approach—from existing data acquisition to 4D model development—that integrates simulation models with advanced ML to create an interactive tool.

Extended Workflow & Project Phases

Phase 1: Preliminary Data Acquisition & Analysis

  • Collect and process existing LiDAR, DEM, and satellite imagery from Google Earth Engine.
  • Harmonize these datasets and establish baseline terrain models.
  • Use published models (e.g., FastScape, Yuan et al. 2019, GoSPL) as initial parameters.

Phase 2: ML Model Development & Simulation

  • Train physics-informed neural networks on the integrated dataset.
  • Calibrate models using existing geological maps of drumlins, moraines, and sediment layers.

Phase 3: 4D Reconstruction & Interactive Visualization

  • Develop a dynamic 4D model (3D spatial + time) that captures both historical evolution and simulated processes (e.g., subglacial floods, sediment flows).
  • Build an immersive interface in Unity for interactive exploration and user-driven scenario testing.

Phase 4: Validation & Scaling

  • Perform targeted field validation with limited sampling.
  • Refine models based on new data and expand simulation capabilities.

Domain-Specific Background

Analyzing glacial landforms requires insights from sedimentology, geomorphology, computational physics, and remote sensing.

Extended Domain Background

Detailed digital terrain analysis shows that the morphology of elliptical mounds (drumlins) encodes crucial information about historical ice dynamics. By coupling high-resolution remote sensing data with numerical simulation techniques, I extract features such as orientation, slope, and curvature—key indicators of sediment transport and subglacial processes.

This interdisciplinary approach enhances my ability to model glacial evolution and predict environmental changes, providing a robust foundation for developing effective remediation strategies.
